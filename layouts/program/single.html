// Methodology

The Approach

Pahim Racing exists at the intersection of elite software engineering and competitive motorsport. The 2027 Tillotson T4 Ireland Championship is a structured performance challenge with measurable inputs, testable hypotheses, and quantifiable outputs.

The objective: demonstrate that professional discipline, open-source methodology, and data-driven iteration can outperform traditional intuition-based racing models. Every variable, from physical conditioning to cornering trajectory, is a parameter to be measured, analyzed, and optimized.

// The Founder

Amador Pahim

Senior Principal Software Engineer at Red Hat, specializing in virtualization, automation, and systems architecture. The same methodology used to design and operate enterprise-scale platforms (rigorous testing, observable systems, iterative improvement) is applied to every aspect of this campaign.

Motorsport is not new territory. Amador competed in a Brazilian state karting championship as a teenager, racing two seasons in a 2-stroke IAME series that built the foundational racecraft now being reactivated. A lifelong passion for racing, shaped by growing up watching Senna, never left. Karting remained a constant through the years, but the discovery of Ireland's Tillotson T4 Championship, a sealed-engine series where engineering discipline outweighs budget, was the catalyst to return to serious competition.
