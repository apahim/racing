PRODUCTION DEPLOYMENT

2027 marks the transition from preparation to production. A new chapter where every foundation-phase investment is brought to the next level—professional telemetry, a racing team, and the Tillotson T4 Ireland Championship.

// The Championship

The Campaign

The Tillotson T4 Ireland Championship is the benchmark series for sealed-engine karting in Ireland. The Senior 168 class sets a 168kg combined kart-and-driver minimum, creating a level playing field where engineering methodology and driver optimization are the only differentiators.

This is a rookie season—framed as a learning deployment, not a title bid. Year one is about data collection, system calibration, and understanding the competitive environment. The goal is to build a baseline, not to win a championship.

› Senior 168 class—168kg combined minimum, sealed engine, mono-brand chassis
› Target circuits: Kiltorcan, Whiteriver Park, Watergrasshill, Athboy
› Season runs March through September 2027
// The Team

Stapleton Motorsport

A rookie campaign requires more than ambition—it requires the competitive infrastructure that only an established team can provide. Stapleton Motorsport brings chassis setup expertise, race-day support, and the operational knowledge that turns a prepared driver into a competitive entry.

The team provides the framework: kart preparation, setup optimization for different track conditions, and the race-weekend logistics that let the driver focus entirely on performance. For an engineer entering motorsport, this is the equivalent of joining a mature platform team rather than building from scratch.

// Telemetry Evolution

The Upgrade

The preparation phase used GoPro footage and BoxBox for post-session analysis. Competition demands real-time, on-kart data. The Alfano 7 professional data logger is the industry standard for karting telemetry—purpose-built hardware replacing the improvised preparation stack.

› Real-time RPM—engine performance monitoring during sessions, not post-session extraction
› GPS lap timing—integrated sector splits and lap delta displayed live on the steering column
› Exhaust gas temperature—engine health and jetting optimization in real-time
› G-force sensors—cornering loads, braking intensity, and traction data
